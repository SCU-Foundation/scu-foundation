SCU Ecosystem Overview

The SCU ecosystem consists of several independent entities with clearly defined roles. This separation preserves neutrality, governance integrity, and long term stability across the SCU architectural definition and the Lattice Protocol.

SCU Foundation

The SCU Foundation is the governance root of the ecosystem. It maintains open standards, preserves canonical publications, and ensures long term institutional clarity.

  • Stewardship of the SCU architectural definition
  • Governance of the Lattice Protocol
  • Publication of canonical documents, version histories, and institutional notes
  • Maintenance of funding transparency and multi year continuity
  • Preservation of archival access to all historical editions
  • Institutional neutrality and vendor agnostic governance

The Foundation does not direct implementations or commercial usage. Its role is to maintain clarity, stability, and openness across the standards it stewards.

Post Silicon Collective

The Post Silicon Collective (PSC) is an open research community focused on exploratory work, discussion, and long term thinking around post silicon computation.

  • Community driven exploration and discussion
  • Independent from the Foundation
  • Open participation and broad inquiry
  • No governance or publication authority

PSC provides a space for exploration without influencing the canonical standards.

Lattice Research Institute

The Lattice Research Institute (LRI) conducts independent research related to semantic infrastructure, trust systems, and the broader Lattice ecosystem.

  • Independent research and analysis
  • Long term conceptual and theoretical work
  • No governance authority over the Lattice Protocol

Why Separation Matters

Clear separation between governance, research, and community exploration prevents conflicts of interest and preserves the neutrality of the standards.

  • Foundation — governance, standards, publications
  • PSC — open community exploration
  • LRI — independent research

This structure ensures that the SCU architecture and the Lattice Protocol remain open, stable, and institutionally trustworthy.
