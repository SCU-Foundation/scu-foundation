Transparency

The SCU Foundation maintains a clear and open accounting of its operational model. During the pre incorporation phase, all core expenses are funded by the founder. This page provides a detailed breakdown of current costs, service providers, and the principles that guide the Foundation's financial transparency.

Current Cost Structure

The Foundation operates with intentionally minimal expenses to ensure long term sustainability during its early development. These costs support the stability, accessibility, and continuity of the SCU ecosystem.

Estimated Annual Costs
• Domain registrations
• Hosting and CDN services
• Email and administrative infrastructure
• Archival storage and redundancy
• Certificates, security, and monitoring
• Minimal stewardship support for ecosystem maintenance

Total estimated cost: approximately $500 AUD per year.

These costs are currently covered by the founder. The Foundation does not accept general donations, but purpose specific contributions may support its formalisation into a non profit organisation.

Service Providers

To support transparency and independent verification, the Foundation lists the service providers used for core infrastructure. All pricing can be confirmed through each provider's public documentation.

  • Domain Registration: Porkbun
  • Hosting Infrastructure: VentraIP and Synergy Wholesale
  • CDN: Cloudflare (free tier)
  • Certificates and Security: Let's Encrypt (current), Cloudflare (planned)
  • Email Infrastructure: cPanel (included with hosting)
  • Archival Storage: Microsoft SharePoint (approximately 75 USD per year)
  • Monitoring: UptimeRobot

Founder Funded Operations

All operational costs are currently funded by the founder. This ensures neutrality and avoids any external influence during the Foundation's early development.

Purpose specific contributions may be accepted to support the formalisation process, including legal registration and the establishment of audited financial structures. These contributions are used exclusively for formalisation and are not applied to general operations.

To discuss supporting formalisation, please contact:
contact@scu.foundation

Transparency Principles

The Foundation's approach to transparency is guided by its governance philosophy: clarity, neutrality, minimalism, and long term stewardship. These principles ensure that all financial activity, current and future, is conducted openly and responsibly.

Once formalised as a non profit organisation, the Foundation will introduce audited reporting and jurisdiction appropriate financial disclosures while maintaining its commitment to global accessibility and institutional neutrality.
