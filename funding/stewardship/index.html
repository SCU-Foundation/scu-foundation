Stewardship Policy

The SCU Foundation follows a conservative, neutrality driven approach to resource management. This policy outlines how the Foundation handles future surplus, maintains independence, and ensures that all resources are used solely to support open standards and long term ecosystem continuity.

Stewardship Principles

The Foundation's stewardship model is grounded in its governance philosophy: minimalism, neutrality, transparency, and long term public benefit. These principles ensure that all resources, current and future, are managed responsibly and without commercial influence.

  • Neutrality: No funding source may influence standards, governance, or ecosystem direction.
  • Transparency: All financial structures and reporting will be publicly accessible once formalised.
  • Minimalism: Operational costs remain intentionally low to ensure sustainability.
  • Public Benefit: All resources support open standards, governance, and long term stewardship.

Surplus Handling

Once the Foundation is formally incorporated and able to receive grants or institutional funding, any surplus will be reinvested directly into the organisation's mission. Surplus funds will never be distributed to individuals, sponsors, or commercial entities.

Surplus may be allocated to:

• Long term archival and documentation infrastructure
• Standards development and governance work
• Public sector alignment and interoperability initiatives
• Safety, compliance, and institutional continuity
• Expansion of ecosystem infrastructure

This ensures that all resources strengthen the Foundation's long term independence and public benefit mission.

Independence and Non Commercial Policy

The Foundation does not accept sponsorships, advertising, or funding that could compromise neutrality. All future funding, whether through grants or purpose specific contributions, must align with the Foundation's governance principles and public benefit mission.

The Foundation will not enter commercial partnerships or agreements that influence standards, governance, or ecosystem direction.

Founder Contributions

During the pre incorporation phase, all operational costs are funded by the founder. These contributions are considered part of the Foundation's early stewardship and do not create any ownership, financial entitlement, or preferential influence.

This ensures that the Foundation's governance and future funding remain fully independent and aligned with public benefit.

Future Reporting and Accountability

Once incorporated as a non profit organisation, the Foundation will introduce audited financial reporting, annual disclosures, and jurisdiction appropriate compliance practices. These reports will be made publicly available to ensure transparency and institutional accountability.

The Foundation will maintain a clear separation between operational costs, formalisation expenses, and grant funded initiatives.

Relationship to Funding

The stewardship policy governs how all future resources are managed. It ensures that funding, whether through grants or purpose specific contributions, supports the Foundation's mission without compromising neutrality or independence.
