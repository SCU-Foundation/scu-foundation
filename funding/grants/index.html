Grants

The SCU Foundation will pursue grant compatible funding channels once formally incorporated as a non profit organisation. This page outlines the types of grants the Foundation intends to seek, the principles guiding its funding strategy, and how future grant support will strengthen long term stewardship of open computational standards.

Grant Strategy

The Foundation's long term funding model is centred on grants rather than general donations or sponsorships. This approach preserves neutrality, avoids commercial influence, and aligns with the Foundation's mission as a governance root standards body.

Once incorporated, the Foundation will pursue grants that support:

• Open standards development
• Public sector alignment and interoperability work
• Safety, governance, and long term continuity initiatives
• Archival and documentation infrastructure
• Institutional stewardship of open computational standards

Grant Eligibility

The Foundation will become eligible for grants only after formal non profit registration. This ensures that all funding is received through audited, jurisdiction appropriate financial structures and that the Foundation meets the compliance requirements of public sector and philanthropic institutions.

Eligibility will depend on:

• Non profit incorporation
• Transparent governance and reporting
• Clear public benefit mission
• Demonstrated stewardship of open standards
• Alignment with grant issuing bodies' objectives

These criteria will be fully met once the Foundation completes its formalisation process.

Types of Grants the Foundation May Pursue

After incorporation, the Foundation will seek grants from institutions that support open standards, public infrastructure, and long term governance. Potential categories include:

  • Public sector grants: Government programs supporting digital infrastructure, safety, and interoperability.
  • Academic and institutional grants: Funding aligned with open standards, governance, and documentation work.
  • Philanthropic grants: Foundations that support public goods, open technology, and long term stewardship.
  • International standards funding: Programs that support global interoperability and open governance frameworks.

These funding sources align with the Foundation's mission and ensure that all support reinforces neutrality and long term public benefit.

Neutrality and Independence

The Foundation does not accept sponsorships, commercial influence, or funding that could compromise neutrality. All future grants will be evaluated against strict governance criteria to ensure alignment with the Foundation's mission and independence.

This approach ensures that the SCU ecosystem remains open, globally accessible, and free from proprietary or commercial control.

Relationship to Formalisation

Grant eligibility depends on the Foundation's transition into a legally recognised non profit organisation. Purpose specific contributions may support this formalisation process, enabling the Foundation to establish the audited financial structures required for grant applications.
