Formalisation Roadmap

The SCU Foundation intends to transition from a founder funded initiative into a formally registered non profit organisation. This roadmap outlines the steps required for incorporation, the principles guiding the transition, and how purpose specific contributions support the process.

Why Formalisation Matters

Formalising the Foundation as a non profit organisation is essential for long term neutrality, continuity, and institutional legitimacy. Incorporation enables the Foundation to:

• Receive grants and public sector funding
• Establish audited financial structures
• Strengthen governance and accountability
• Ensure long term independence from any individual
• Provide a stable home for open standards stewardship

These capabilities are necessary for the Foundation to operate as a globally trusted standards body.

Current Phase

The Foundation is currently in its pre incorporation phase. During this period, all operational costs are funded by the founder, and the organisation maintains a minimal, transparent cost structure.

Purpose specific contributions may support the formalisation process, including legal registration, administrative setup, and the establishment of audited financial channels.

Formalisation Steps

The transition to a non profit organisation involves several structured steps. These steps ensure that the Foundation meets jurisdictional requirements and maintains its commitment to neutrality and public benefit.

  1. Governance Stabilisation
    Finalise the governance model, standards architecture, and institutional documentation.
  2. Jurisdiction Selection
    Evaluate jurisdictions suitable for international non profits, based on legal clarity, administrative efficiency, and long term stability.
  3. Legal Registration
    Complete the formal incorporation process, including legal filings, registration fees, and administrative setup.
  4. Financial Infrastructure
    Establish audited, jurisdiction appropriate financial channels for receiving grants and managing institutional funds.
  5. Compliance and Reporting
    Implement statutory reporting, annual disclosures, and governance aligned transparency practices.
  6. Grant Eligibility
    Once incorporated, begin applying for public sector, academic, and philanthropic grants.

Jurisdiction Considerations

The Foundation is evaluating jurisdictions known for supporting international non profits, digital governance organisations, and open standards institutions. Key considerations include:

• Legal clarity and stability
• International recognition
• Administrative efficiency
• Cost of incorporation and ongoing compliance
• Suitability for grant eligibility

A final jurisdiction will be selected based on these criteria and the Foundation's long term mission.

How Contributions Support Formalisation

Purpose specific contributions directly support the Foundation's transition into a non profit organisation. These contributions are used exclusively for:

• Legal registration fees
• Administrative setup
• Compliance and reporting infrastructure
• Establishing audited financial channels

To discuss supporting the formalisation process, please contact:
contact@scu.foundation

Long Term Vision

Once formalised, the SCU Foundation will operate as a neutral, globally accessible standards body dedicated to the stewardship of open computational frameworks. Incorporation ensures that the Foundation's mission continues independently of any individual and remains aligned with public benefit.

This transition marks the beginning of a long term institutional commitment to openness, safety, and global interoperability.
