Funding and Formalisation

The SCU Foundation operates with a minimal, transparent cost structure during its pre incorporation phase. Core operational expenses are currently funded by the founder. The Foundation does not accept general donations, but it does welcome purpose specific contributions that support its formalisation into a neutral, non profit standards body.

Current Funding Model

The Foundation is presently in its pre incorporation phase. All operational costs, including domains, hosting, archival storage, certificates, and essential infrastructure, are funded directly by the founder. These costs are intentionally minimal and focused on maintaining the stability and accessibility of the SCU ecosystem.

To preserve neutrality, the Foundation does not accept open ended or general donations. However, it does accept purpose specific contributions that directly support the formalisation process, including legal registration, non profit establishment, and the creation of audited financial structures.

Current Annual Costs
• Domain registrations
• Hosting and CDN services
• Email and administrative infrastructure
• Archival storage and redundancy
• Certificates, security, and monitoring
• Minimal stewardship support for ecosystem maintenance

Total estimated cost: approximately $500 AUD per year across all ecosystem surfaces.

Support Formalisation

Formalising the SCU Foundation as a non profit organisation requires legal registration, administrative setup, and jurisdiction appropriate compliance. These steps involve costs that exceed the Foundation's current self funded operating budget.

Individuals or organisations who wish to support the Foundation's transition into a fully incorporated, grant eligible institution may contribute specifically to the formalisation process. All such contributions are used exclusively for:

• Non profit registration fees
• Legal and administrative setup
• Initial compliance and reporting infrastructure
• Establishing audited financial channels

To discuss supporting the formalisation process, please contact:
contact@scu.foundation

Transparency

The Foundation maintains a clear and open accounting of its operational model. While general donations are not accepted, the Foundation discloses its cost structure, service providers, and stewardship principles to ensure institutional clarity.

A detailed breakdown of infrastructure, providers, and cost categories is available on the Transparency page →

Future Grants and Funding

Once formalised as a non profit organisation, the Foundation will pursue grant compatible funding channels to support long term stewardship, governance, and public good maintenance. These may include:

• Public sector or academic partnerships
• Philanthropic or institutional funding
• Standards body support programs
• Research aligned grants administered through appropriate partners

These mechanisms will only be introduced once the Foundation is legally incorporated and able to maintain audited, jurisdiction appropriate financial structures.

Learn more about future grants →

Formalisation Pathway

The SCU Foundation intends to formally register as a non profit organisation once the governance model, standards architecture, and ecosystem surfaces reach their stable, long term form. This transition will:

• Establish audited financial reporting
• Enable grant eligibility
• Strengthen institutional neutrality
• Provide long term continuity and legal clarity

Formalisation will not change the Foundation's mission. It will provide the legal and institutional structure required for sustainable, global stewardship.

Read about the formalisation roadmap →

Stewardship and Surplus

The Foundation follows a conservative, stewardship driven approach to resource management. Any future surplus, once formal funding channels exist, will be reinvested directly into open standards, governance, and ecosystem continuity.

The Foundation does not engage in sponsorships, commercial influence, or profit driven activities. All resources are used solely to support the long term health of the SCU ecosystem.
