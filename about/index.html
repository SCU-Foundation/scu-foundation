About the SCU Foundation

The SCU Foundation is the independent standards body responsible for the governance, stewardship, and long term continuity of open computational standards. It maintains institutional neutrality, archival continuity, and global accessibility across the SCU ecosystem, including the SCU architectural definition and the Lattice Protocol.

Standards and Stewardship

The SCU Foundation stewards two primary bodies of work:

• The SCU architectural definition, maintained through the canonical SCU Whitepaper series. The current edition is SCU Whitepaper v1.8, with v1.7 preserved for archival continuity.
• The Lattice Protocol, an open semantic, identity, and trust standard for AI native communication.

The Foundation is institutionally separate from the Lattice Research Institute (semantic research) and the Post Silicon Collective (long horizon exploration). This separation preserves neutrality and ensures that governance, research, and exploratory work remain independent.

Mission

The mission of the SCU Foundation is to steward open standards that support safe, interoperable, and future oriented computational ecosystems. This includes:

• Governance of the SCU architectural definition and its canonical whitepaper series
• Stewardship of the Lattice Protocol as an open, non commercial communication standard
• Preservation of institutional knowledge and archival continuity across the SCU ecosystem

The Foundation ensures that researchers, engineers, and institutions can rely on stable, citable, and openly accessible references as the computational landscape evolves.

Stewardship Philosophy

The SCU Foundation operates with a commitment to openness, clarity, and long term accessibility. All standards and publications are maintained as public goods, independent of any corporation or commercial interest.

Our stewardship philosophy emphasises:

  • Archival stability. Every version of every publication remains permanently accessible.
  • Technical clarity. Documentation is precise, readable, and future proof.
  • Institutional neutrality. No vendor or implementation receives preferential treatment.
  • Open access. All materials are freely available without registration or tracking.

Institutional Principles

The SCU Foundation is guided by principles that ensure long term credibility, transparency, and global accessibility. These principles shape all aspects of our governance and documentation.

  • Clarity. Information is presented in a precise and accessible manner.
  • Neutrality. The Foundation maintains a globally neutral posture.
  • Minimalism. Operations prioritise simplicity and maintainability.
  • Honesty. Processes and financial activity are documented openly.
  • Global accessibility. All standards and publications are available without barriers.
  • Standards body tone. Communication follows a consistent, formal, neutral style.

Governance

The SCU Foundation maintains its standards through a transparent governance model focused on documentation integrity, version control, and long term stewardship. Governance responsibilities include:

  • Stewardship of the Lattice Protocol
  • Maintaining the canonical SCU Whitepaper and its version history
  • Publishing institutional documents and supporting materials
  • Ensuring consistency across all Foundation publications
  • Preserving long term accessibility and archival stability
