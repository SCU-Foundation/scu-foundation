SCU Foundation

The SCU Foundation is the independent standards body responsible for the governance, stewardship, and long term continuity of open computational standards. It maintains the SCU architectural definition and oversees the Lattice Protocol, ensuring neutrality, clarity, and global accessibility across the SCU ecosystem.

Canonical Standards

• SCU Whitepaper v1.8 (current canonical edition)
• SCU Whitepaper v1.7 (archived for continuity)
• Lattice Protocol (open semantic, identity, and trust substrate)

Mission

The SCU Foundation provides independent, long term governance for open standards that support safe, interoperable, and future oriented computational ecosystems. Its mission includes:

• Stewardship of the SCU architectural definition
• Governance of the Lattice Protocol as an open, non commercial communication standard
• Maintenance of institutional publications, archival records, and governance frameworks that ensure clarity, neutrality, and continuity

Governance

The SCU Foundation operates a transparent, stability focused governance model. It is institutionally separate from the Lattice Research Institute (semantic research) and the Post Silicon Collective (long horizon exploration) to preserve neutrality and prevent category confusion.

Ecosystem

The SCU ecosystem comprises the protocol substrate, the semantic research substrate, and the long horizon exploration substrate that together support open, interoperable computation.

• Lattice Protocol (protocol substrate)
• Lattice Research Institute (semantic substrate)
• Post Silicon Collective (exploration substrate)

Funding and Transparency

The SCU Foundation maintains transparent funding and stewardship practices. The Foundation seeks funding only for governance root operations, legal formalisation, and long term stewardship. Research funding is pursued independently by the Lattice Research Institute.
