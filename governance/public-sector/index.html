Public Sector Alignment

Public Sector Alignment defines how governments and institutions adopt the Lattice Protocol and the SCU architectural definition. It ensures that public sector deployments meet strict identity, safety, and semantic requirements while benefiting from neutrality and long term stability.

Purpose of Public Sector Alignment

Public Sector Alignment ensures that governments and institutions can adopt the Lattice Protocol as a trusted public standard. It defines the requirements, guarantees, and governance processes that support safe and interoperable public sector deployments.

This framework supports long term institutional trust by ensuring that the protocol remains neutral, stable, and free from commercial influence.

Governance Bodies Involved

Public Sector Alignment operates across the three governance bodies defined in the Institutional Structure.

  • SCU Foundation. Defines public sector requirements, certification rules, and long term governance guarantees.
  • Lattice Research Institute. Provides semantic research and interpretability frameworks that support public sector safety requirements.
  • Post Silicon Collective. Publishes long horizon signals that may inform future public sector considerations.

Public Sector Requirements

Public sector deployments must meet strict requirements across identity, safety, semantics, and trust. These requirements ensure that the protocol can be used in high accountability environments.

  • Identity guarantees. All agents and devices must operate under verifiable identity and provenance.
  • Safety guarantees. Deployments must comply with the Safety Governance framework.
  • Semantic stability. Meaning must remain consistent across agents, domains, and institutions.
  • Interoperability. Public sector systems must interoperate with certified agents and devices.
  • Transparency. Deployments must support auditability and public accountability.

Certification for Public Sector Deployments

Public sector deployments require certification to ensure compliance with identity, safety, and semantic requirements. Certification rules are defined by the SCU Foundation and implemented through the Lattice Protocol certification framework.

Certification includes:

  • agent certification
  • device certification
  • runtime certification
  • extension certification
  • attestation and provenance verification

Long Term Stability

Public sector adoption requires long term stability. The SCU Foundation ensures stability through:

  • strict versioning rules defined in the Versioning Governance framework
  • permanent archival access to all editions
  • transparent change logs
  • neutral governance processes

These guarantees support institutional trust and long term adoption.

Alignment with Change Management

Public sector requirements influence how proposals are evaluated through the Change Management framework. Proposals must demonstrate that they support or improve public sector viability.

This ensures that the protocol evolves in a way that remains compatible with high accountability environments.
