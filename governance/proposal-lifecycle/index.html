Proposal Lifecycle

The Proposal Lifecycle defines how research proposals move from the Lattice Research Institute through evaluation, standardization, and publication within the SCU Foundation governance model. It ensures that changes to the SCU architectural definition and the Lattice Protocol follow transparent, documented, and institutionally neutral processes.

Purpose of the Proposal Lifecycle

The Proposal Lifecycle ensures that all changes to the SCU architectural definition and the Lattice Protocol follow a clear and predictable process. It defines how research becomes a proposal, how proposals are evaluated, and how accepted changes are incorporated into new editions of the standards.

This lifecycle supports neutrality, stability, and long term governance by separating research, evaluation, and exploratory signals across the three governance bodies.

Lifecycle Overview

The lifecycle consists of four stages that move from research to publication:

  1. Research. Conducted by the Lattice Research Institute.
  2. Evaluation. Performed by the SCU Foundation.
  3. Standardization. Accepted proposals are incorporated into new editions.
  4. Publication. The updated standard is published with full metadata and archival access.

Long horizon signals from the Post Silicon Collective may inform future research and governance decisions.

Stage 1. Research

Research begins at the Lattice Research Institute. The Institute explores new semantic constructs, packet formats, interpretability frameworks, and protocol behaviours. Research outputs are documented as proposals.

Proposals must include:

  • a clear problem statement
  • a description of the proposed change
  • semantic and safety implications
  • expected interoperability impact
  • reference examples or diagrams when relevant

Stage 2. Evaluation

The SCU Foundation evaluates proposals using the governance frameworks defined in:

  • Change Management
  • Versioning Governance
  • Safety Governance
  • Public Sector Alignment

Evaluation criteria include:

  • semantic stability
  • safety and interpretability
  • interoperability impact
  • alignment with the Conceptual Frame
  • compatibility with the Architecture Model

Stage 3. Standardization

Accepted proposals are incorporated into new editions of the SCU architectural definition or the Lattice Protocol. Each edition is:

  • stable
  • citable
  • archived with complete metadata
  • published with a clear change log

Standardization follows the rules defined in the Versioning Governance framework.

Stage 4. Publication

Once standardized, the updated edition is published as a public standard. Publication includes:

  • HTML and PDF editions
  • archival access to all previous versions
  • a complete change log
  • updated diagrams and supporting materials

Publication ensures global accessibility and long term stability.

Long Horizon Signals

The Post Silicon Collective publishes long horizon research signals related to post silicon compute, cryogenic architectures, and future computational substrates. These signals do not change the protocol directly but may inform future research at the Lattice Research Institute.
