Versioning Governance

Versioning Governance defines how editions of the SCU architectural definition and the Lattice Protocol are created, maintained, deprecated, and archived. It ensures stability, safety, and long term interoperability across the ecosystem.

Purpose of Versioning Governance

Versioning Governance ensures that all editions of the SCU architectural definition and the Lattice Protocol follow a predictable and transparent lifecycle. It defines how new editions are created, how changes are recorded, and how older versions are preserved for archival and citation purposes.

This framework protects stability and prevents fragmentation by ensuring that all changes follow the Proposal Lifecycle and the Change Management process.

Versioning Principles

All versioning decisions follow six core principles:

  • Stability. Published editions are never altered.
  • Transparency. All changes are documented with clear metadata.
  • Neutrality. No commercial entity influences versioning decisions.
  • Safety. Changes must maintain or improve safety guarantees.
  • Semantic Integrity. The meaning of protocol constructs must remain coherent across editions.
  • Archival Continuity. All editions remain permanently accessible.

Edition Types

The SCU Foundation publishes three types of editions:

  • Major Editions. Introduce significant conceptual or architectural changes. Require full governance review.
  • Minor Editions. Introduce clarifications, safety improvements, or incremental semantic updates.
  • Patch Editions. Correct errors in diagrams, metadata, or formatting without altering meaning.

All edition types follow the same archival and publication rules.

Versioning Flow

The versioning flow follows the governance structure defined in the Institutional Structure.

  1. Research. The Lattice Research Institute develops proposals.
  2. Evaluation. The SCU Foundation evaluates proposals using the Change Management framework.
  3. Edition Planning. Accepted proposals are grouped into a planned edition.
  4. Publication. The new edition is published with full metadata and archival access.
  5. Deprecation. Older editions may be deprecated but remain permanently accessible.

Archival Rules

All editions are archived permanently. Archival rules include:

  • HTML and PDF editions for every version
  • permanent URLs for all historical editions
  • complete change logs
  • metadata including publication date, edition type, and governance notes

Archival continuity ensures that researchers, institutions, and public sector bodies can rely on stable references.

Deprecation

Deprecation indicates that an edition is no longer recommended for new implementations. Deprecated editions remain accessible for historical, academic, and interoperability purposes.

Deprecation decisions follow the Change Management process and require clear justification.

Role of External Governance Bodies

Versioning Governance interacts with the other governance bodies as follows:

  • Lattice Research Institute. Submits proposals that may lead to new editions.
  • Post Silicon Collective. Provides long horizon signals that may influence future editions.
