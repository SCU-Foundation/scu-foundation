Transparency

Transparency ensures that governance decisions, funding information, proposal outcomes, and archival records are publicly accessible. It supports institutional trust, neutrality, and long term accountability across the SCU ecosystem.

Purpose of Transparency

Transparency ensures that the SCU Foundation operates with clarity and openness. It defines what information is published, how it is maintained, and how the public can verify governance decisions and funding structures.

Transparency supports neutrality by ensuring that no commercial or private influence can shape the standards without public visibility.

Governance Bodies and Transparency

Transparency applies across the three governance bodies defined in the Institutional Structure.

  • SCU Foundation. Publishes governance decisions, funding information, edition metadata, and archival records.
  • Lattice Research Institute. Publishes research outputs, proposals, and semantic documentation.
  • Post Silicon Collective. Publishes long horizon research signals.

Funding Transparency

The SCU Foundation publishes clear information about its funding sources and responsibilities. The Foundation seeks funding only for governance root operations, legal formalisation, and long term stewardship.

The Foundation does not seek or manage research funding. Research grants are pursued independently by the Lattice Research Institute.

Funding transparency includes:

  • annual funding summaries
  • public reporting of grant support
  • clear separation between governance funding and research funding
  • public disclosure of operational costs

The Foundation publishes only governance root funding information. Research funding is disclosed independently by the Lattice Research Institute.

Proposal Transparency

All proposals submitted through the Proposal Lifecycle are published with:

  • proposal identifiers
  • submission date
  • research body attribution
  • evaluation notes
  • decision outcome
  • edition assignment when applicable

This ensures that the evolution of the standards is fully visible to the public.

Decision Transparency

All governance decisions made through the Change Management process are recorded and published. This includes:

  • accepted proposals
  • deferred proposals
  • rejected proposals
  • rationale for each decision

Decision transparency ensures that governance remains accountable and institutionally neutral.

Edition Transparency

All editions of the SCU architectural definition and the Lattice Protocol are published with complete metadata, including:

  • edition number
  • publication date
  • edition type
  • change log
  • archival links

Edition transparency supports long term stability and public sector adoption.

Archival Transparency

All historical editions remain permanently accessible. Archival transparency ensures that researchers, institutions, and public sector bodies can verify the evolution of the standards.

Archival rules are defined in the Versioning Governance framework.
