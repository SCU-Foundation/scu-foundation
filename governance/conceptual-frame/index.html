Conceptual Frame

The Conceptual Frame defines the purpose, scope, and category of the SCU ecosystem and the Lattice Protocol. It establishes the conceptual substrate on which governance, standards, semantics, and ecosystem structure are built.

Purpose of the Conceptual Frame

The Conceptual Frame explains why the SCU ecosystem exists and what problem it solves. It defines the category of the Lattice Protocol, the role of governance, and the boundaries that separate the protocol from implementations, products, and commercial activity.

This document sits above all technical layers and governance processes. It provides the conceptual grounding for the Architecture Model, the Lattice Protocol, and the work of the Lattice Research Institute and the Post Silicon Collective.

Category Definition

The SCU ecosystem defines a new category of infrastructure for the AI native internet. This category is built on identity, semantics, safety, and trust. It is not a product, a platform, or a commercial service. It is a public standard that enables safe and interoperable computation across agents, devices, and institutions.

The Lattice Protocol is the semantic and identity substrate of this category. It defines how meaning, identity, provenance, and capability access are represented and exchanged between AI systems.

Boundaries

The Conceptual Frame establishes clear boundaries that preserve neutrality and prevent category confusion.

  • Governance boundary. The SCU Foundation governs standards, not implementations.
  • Semantic boundary. The Lattice Research Institute develops semantics, not products.
  • Exploration boundary. The Post Silicon Collective explores long horizon compute, not protocol changes.
  • Commercial boundary. No commercial entity controls or directs the standards.
  • Protocol boundary. The protocol defines identity, semantics, and trust, not application behaviour.

These boundaries ensure that the ecosystem remains neutral, stable, and globally adoptable.

Relationship to the Architecture Model

The Conceptual Frame defines the purpose and category of the ecosystem. The Architecture Model defines its structure. Together, they form the governance root of the SCU ecosystem.

The Conceptual Frame explains why the ecosystem exists. The Architecture Model explains how it is organised.

Governance Bodies

The Conceptual Frame defines the roles and boundaries of the three governance bodies.

  • SCU Foundation. Governance and stewardship of the SCU architectural definition and the Lattice Protocol.
  • Lattice Research Institute. Semantic research and development of the Lattice Language.
  • Post Silicon Collective. Exploration of post silicon compute and long horizon signals.

These bodies operate independently to preserve neutrality and public good stewardship.
