Architecture Model

The Architecture Model describes the seven layer structure of the AI native internet. It defines how governance, standards, semantics, trust, execution, data ingestion, and applications relate to one another. This model provides the canonical map that positions the SCU architectural definition, the Lattice Protocol, and the broader ecosystem.

Purpose of the Architecture Model

The Architecture Model establishes the structural foundation for the SCU ecosystem. It defines the boundaries, responsibilities, and interactions between layers, ensuring that governance, semantics, and technical standards evolve in a coherent and future oriented manner.

This model sits above all technical specifications and informs how the SCU Foundation, the Lattice Research Institute, and the Post Silicon Collective operate within the ecosystem.

For the conceptual framing of the ecosystem, see the Conceptual Frame.

The Seven Layers of the AI Native Internet

The Architecture Model defines seven layers that together form the AI native internet. Each layer has a distinct purpose and boundary.

  1. Governance Root. Defines institutional authority, versioning rules, safety requirements, certification regimes, and public sector alignment. Governed by the SCU Foundation.
  2. Protocol Layer. The canonical specification of the Lattice Protocol. Defines identity, semantics, trust, packet formats, and capability access.
  3. Semantic Layer. Research and development of the Lattice Language, semantic structures, and interpretability frameworks. Led by the Lattice Research Institute.
  4. Trust Layer. Certification, attestation, provenance, and identity guarantees. Ensures that agents, devices, and runtimes operate under verifiable trust conditions.
  5. Execution Layer. Agents, runtimes, and execution environments that implement the protocol. Includes certified devices and attested compute substrates.
  6. Data Ingestion Layer. Perceptual inputs, domain separation, and safety boundaries that govern how agents receive and process information.
  7. Application Layer. User facing systems, tools, and integrations that rely on the protocol and its guarantees.

These layers form a coherent architecture that supports identity, safety, semantics, and trust across the AI native internet.

Relationship to Governance

Governance sits above and around all layers of the Architecture Model. It defines how standards evolve, how safety is enforced, how certification works, and how public sector adoption is supported.

Ecosystem Bodies

The Architecture Model defines how the three governance bodies operate across the layers.

  • SCU Foundation. Governs the protocol, maintains canonical specifications, manages certification, and ensures long term neutrality.
  • Lattice Research Institute. Conducts semantic research, develops the Lattice Language, and submits proposals for future editions of the protocol.
  • Post Silicon Collective. Explores post silicon compute and publishes long horizon signals that may inform future governance.

These bodies operate independently to preserve neutrality and public good stewardship.
