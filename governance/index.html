Governance

The SCU Foundation governs the SCU architectural definition and the Lattice Protocol through a transparent, stability focused, and institutionally neutral governance model. Governance ensures that these standards remain consistent, citable, and globally accessible as long term public goods.

Purpose of Governance

Governance defines how the SCU architectural definition and the Lattice Protocol evolve over time. It ensures that changes are evaluated through open, documented processes and that the standards remain neutral, stable, and globally adoptable.

The Foundation does not direct implementations or commercial usage. Its role is to maintain the integrity, clarity, and accessibility of the standards themselves, ensuring they remain open and vendor agnostic.

All governance decisions follow the Foundation's institutional principles: clarity, neutrality, minimalism, honesty, global accessibility, and a consistent standards body tone.

For the conceptual and architectural foundations of governance, see the Conceptual Frame and the Architecture Model.

SCU Governance Bodies

Governance of the SCU architectural definition and the Lattice Protocol is carried out by three non commercial, structurally independent bodies. Each has a distinct mandate and boundary.

  • SCU Foundation. Governance and stewardship. Maintains canonical specifications, certification rules, versioning, archival continuity, and long term governance.
  • Lattice Research Institute. Semantic research. Explores the Lattice Language, semantic structures, packet formats, and the Lattice Web. Submits proposals for future editions of the protocol.
  • Post Silicon Collective. Exploration and signals. Conducts long horizon research into post silicon compute and publishes ecosystem signals that may inform future governance.

These bodies operate independently and are insulated from commercial influence to preserve neutrality and public good stewardship.

The SCU Foundation does not seek or manage research funding. Research grants are pursued independently by the Lattice Research Institute.

How Governance Works

Governance follows a clear, layered flow from research to standard to global adoption.

  1. Research and Proposals. The Lattice Research Institute explores new semantic constructs, packet formats, and protocol behaviours. Proposals are documented and submitted to the SCU Foundation. See the Proposal Lifecycle.
  2. Evaluation and Standardization. The SCU Foundation evaluates proposals against safety, interoperability, semantic stability, and governance criteria. Accepted proposals are incorporated into new editions of the standards. See Change Management.
  3. Publication. The canonical specifications are published as public standards with clear versioning, change logs, and deprecation policies. See Versioning Governance.
  4. Ecosystem Signals. The Post Silicon Collective provides long term research signals and ecosystem insights that may inform future governance decisions.

Scope of Governance

Governance covers the full lifecycle of the SCU architectural definition and the Lattice Protocol, including:

  • Standards stewardship
  • Safety governance
  • Public sector alignment
  • Versioning and deprecation
  • Institutional structure
  • Transparency

Institutional Neutrality

The SCU Foundation does not endorse specific vendors, implementations, or commercial products. Its role is to maintain open standards that can be adopted, implemented, and extended by any organisation without restriction.
