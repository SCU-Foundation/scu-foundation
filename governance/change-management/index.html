Change Management

Change Management defines how proposals are evaluated, accepted, or rejected within the SCU Foundation governance model. It ensures that all changes to the SCU architectural definition and the Lattice Protocol follow transparent, documented, and safety aligned processes.

Purpose of Change Management

Change Management ensures that updates to the SCU architectural definition and the Lattice Protocol are evaluated consistently and responsibly. It defines how proposals are reviewed, how decisions are made, and how accepted changes move into new editions.

This framework protects semantic stability, safety, and interoperability across the ecosystem.

Governance Bodies Involved

Change Management operates across the three governance bodies defined in the Institutional Structure.

  • SCU Foundation. Evaluates proposals and makes final decisions.
  • Lattice Research Institute. Submits research proposals.
  • Post Silicon Collective. Provides long horizon signals that may inform evaluation.

Evaluation Criteria

All proposals are evaluated using consistent criteria to ensure safety, stability, and alignment with the governance root.

  • Semantic stability. The proposal must preserve or improve semantic coherence.
  • Safety. The proposal must maintain or strengthen safety guarantees.
  • Interoperability. The proposal must not break existing deployments.
  • Conceptual alignment. The proposal must align with the Conceptual Frame.
  • Architectural compatibility. The proposal must fit within the Architecture Model.
  • Public sector viability. The proposal must support long term institutional adoption.

Decision Outcomes

Each proposal results in one of three outcomes:

  • Accepted. The proposal moves into edition planning.
  • Deferred. The proposal requires further research or clarification.
  • Rejected. The proposal does not meet governance criteria.

All decisions are documented with clear justification and archived for transparency.

Change Log Requirements

Accepted proposals must include a complete change log entry that documents:

  • the proposal identifier
  • a summary of the change
  • semantic and safety implications
  • interoperability considerations
  • edition impact

Change logs are published alongside each edition as part of the Versioning Governance framework.

Integration with Versioning

Change Management feeds directly into edition planning. Accepted proposals are grouped into major, minor, or patch editions according to the rules defined in the Versioning Governance framework.

This ensures that changes are published in a stable, predictable, and well documented manner.

Transparency

All decisions made through Change Management are recorded and published according to the Transparency framework. This includes:

  • proposal status
  • evaluation notes
  • decision rationale
  • edition assignment
